About the Create Product Wizard

When you create a new product, the Create Product wizard walks you through the creation process step by step showing only those options and fields that are relevant to the type of product you are creating.

Summary of Wizard Steps

The Create Product wizard has a total of nine possible steps, including steps to set up variations. Not every step will be needed for every product. If you are not creating a multiple product with variations you will not have to complete those steps that deal with variations.

When you first enter the wizard you will see a set of indicators at the top of the page that show all nine possible steps of the wizard. The indicators show all nine steps because the wizard does not yet know which template you will choose and therefore which steps you will have to take to complete the product. Once you choose a template and advance to the second step of the wizard, the indicators will update to reflect the steps you will have to take.

A summary of the steps in the Create Product wizard are given below, including what you do in each step is given below. Please note that some steps may not be needed, depending on selections you make in previous steps.

  1. Template

    What you do in this step: Choose whether you want to create an individual or multiple product. Multiple products are products that have variations, such as different versions for operating systems or size and capacity. Individual products are products that do not have variations.

    Note: If you create an individual product, you can later change the product to be a multiple with variations just by adding variations to the product, thus changing the template.

  2. Supported Locales

    What you do in this step: Choose the locales you want the product to support. The locales you select determine the localization you can do on the product name, description, and other product attributes in a later step. If required, you can choose to limit the purchasing of the product to the supported locales. By default, products can be purchased from any locale (within any defined export controls).
  3. Catalogs and Categories

    What you do in this step: Choose the catalog and the categories within the catalog you want to assign to the product. Catalogs and categories are used to organize the product on your site and determine which price list is used with the product.

  4. Product Options

    What you do in this step: Choose the options for the product. There are many different options you can select, based on your company and site. Not all product options are set for every product. You will define the options you have selected here in Step 6: Product Settings.
  5. Fulfillment Options

    What you do in this step: Choose how the product will be fulfilled, that is how it is distributed to shoppers. You can choose to have it physically shipped, downloaded from your store, created on demand (using the Physical on Demand option), or fulfilled using another method.

    Note: If you are creating a multiple product with variations, you can select more than one fulfillment option. However, if you do this, you will have to use those fulfillment options to define the variations. You should only select multiple fulfillment options if the product will vary on how it is fulfilled.

  6. Product Settings

    What you do in this step: Enter information about the product and configure the product and fulfillment options you selected in the previous two steps. There are many different options you may have to set up, depending on the product. For more about the settings you may define in this step, see Product Options.
  7. Variation Options

    What you do in this step: Choose how the base product will vary. This is done by selecting which of the Product Options and possibly which Fulfillment Options you will use to create variations for the product. Variations are not created in this step, you simply choose what attributes will be used to create the variations in the next step.

    Note: This step is only performed when creating a multiple product with variations.
  8. Edit Variations

    What you do in this step: Create the variations for the product by selecting the Product Options and possibly Fulfillment Options that will define the product variation. In this step you also create each individual variation, and enter such details as name, part number, SKU, product image, description, and so on.

    Note: This step is only performed when creating a multiple product with variations.
  9. Pricing

    What you do in this step: Define the pricing for the product in the supported locales/currencies.
