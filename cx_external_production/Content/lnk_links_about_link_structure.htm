Basic Link Structure

Links that take a shopper to a page on your store or perform an action have a specific structure and pattern. There are several elements in the link that indicate what the link does and where the shopper is taken when they click the link. Understanding the structure of links for your store help you create better links and allow you to troubleshoot why your links do not work as expected.

The following shows the basic structure for a link. The bold colored parts vary, based on the store and what the link is intended to do. The first part of the link that is not bold is the same for every link for any store, regardless of what action the link will take.

http://store.digitalriver.com/store/siteID/locale/action/parameter.value

The sample below shows a link created by the Links feature. The link, when clicked, would load the store using a specific theme and locale (Russia) and currency (Ruble, or RUR) and add a certain product to the Shopping Cart. Traffic from this link would be tracked by a program. We have highlighted the part of the URL that can change based on the store, locale, and what the link is intended to do.

http://store.digitalriver.com/store/MySiteID/ru_RU/buy/productID.120932000/quantity.1/pgm.402000/ThemeID.1283100/currency.RUR
