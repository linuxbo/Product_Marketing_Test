Fraud Review Process: An Overview

How does fraud prevention really work and what are the key screening steps in the order process? Let’s examine how Digital River’s best practices apply to an actual order case. The transaction and fraud prevention review processes involve a series of seven steps:

  1. Customer initiates an online transaction and is seamlessly redirected to a secure web page hosted by Digital River. There, the customer selects items for purchase and submits a credit card for processing. The customer is prompted to enter a name, email address, billing and shipping information, credit card information, and any other necessary information required to process the sale.
  2. Upon completion of the order process, the customer clicks the "Submit" or "Pay Now" button on the order confirmation page. The payment process begins.
  3. The "Submit" or "Pay Now" link transfers the order information to Digital River’s transaction processing servers and subsequently routes the transaction through Digital River’s fraud system for a real-time fraud risk assessment.
  4. Pre-Authorization Fraud Detection – Every data point in the transaction is cross-referenced against historical negative files and given a fraud score based on hundreds of complex parameters patented by Digital River. If the score exceeds a given threshold, the order will be classified as fraudulent and automatically rejected by the system.
  5. Post-Authorization, Pre-Fulfillment Fraud Detection – If the transaction passes through the fraud system successfully, a credit card authorization or declination code is obtained. When a card is successfully authorized, some fraud-related information is obtained from the card association network (AVS, CVN, 3-D Secure, etc.)
  6. Post-Authorization, Post Fulfillment Fraud Detection – If the transaction exceeds certain scoring rules thresholds, it is placed into manual review. Depending on the dollar amount, product type, or other fraud avoidance parameters, the order process will either immediately provide the download or delay the shipment of the product, the download, or generation of the product key. Next, a fraud reviewer manually rejects or releases the order. If the product has not been fulfilled and is rejected, we return to Step 5. If the order is fulfilled but rejected prior to the credit card settlement to avoid a chargeback, it results in post-authorization, post-fulfillment fraud detection. All of these transactions are thoroughly evaluated by a fraud review analyst.
  7. Post-Settlement Fraud Detection with Refund or Chargeback – An order that has survived this scrutiny may still be fraudulent. In this case, the process will be finalized in one of two ways. The customer contacts us and we obtain signed confirmation to reverse the transaction (post-settlement fraud detection with refund), or we obtain a chargeback from our payment processor (post settlement fraud detection with chargeback).
