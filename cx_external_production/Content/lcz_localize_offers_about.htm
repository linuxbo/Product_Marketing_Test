About Localizing Offers

Most stores are set up to support multiple locales. When you create an offer, you may want to set up the offer to also support additional locales. Setting up an offer to support a locale is called localizing the offer or localizing the offer display/content. Localizing an offer allows you to further customize how the offer looks on your store and tailor the offer to a specific locale or audience. It can be important to localize offers because language and currency differences can affect the offer discount and offer content.

When you localize an offer you are simply adding display content (sales pitch text, offer images, and so on) and pricing in the language and currency where you want to the offer to be available. For example, you could localize an offer so that the offer text and pricing support both the English and French locales within Canada. To do this you would simply add descriptive text and images in French and English and set up pricing for the Canadian Dollar (and possibly the United States Dollar).

Note: If the offer you want to localize will discount specific products, you should also localize those products for the locales supported by the offer. If you do this, the shopper will not only see localized offer information, but localized product information such as product description and name.

Offer display content appears in the point of promotion (POP) associated with the offer, so localized content only appears for offers that use a POP. When a shopper views an offer they are seeing the display content entered for the locale they are viewing. If an offer does not use a POP, you cannot enter display content for supported locales, but you can enter content for the default locale.
