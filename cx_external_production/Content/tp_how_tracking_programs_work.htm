How Tracking Programs Work

A tracking program is really just a code that you add to links created for your store. When a link with a tracking program ID in it is clicked, that click and the activity resulting from that click is recorded. In this sense, tracking programs are really nothing more than a way to track how often links are clicked for your store.

Tracking programs only track the last click prior to a purchase. This means that if a shopper clicks a tracking program link to enter your store, but clicks another tracking program link while in your store and then completes a purchase, their order will be associated with the second click, not the first.

There is no limit to the number of tracking programs you can create and the creative ways you can use them with links to track products, offers, store traffic, marketing campaigns, etc.

Tip: You can use a tracking program to track offers that do not provide a discount, since offers without a discount are not tracked by the standard offer reports.
