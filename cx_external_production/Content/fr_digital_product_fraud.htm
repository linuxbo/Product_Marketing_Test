Fraud and Digital Products

Digital products pose an especially high risk for fraudulent activity. The delivery system does not provide a way to "face the consumer" at any stage of the transaction. The acquisition of a downloadable product is instantaneous, which makes the decision to accept or reject an order a real-time or "near" real-time requirement.

Online transactions of digital products include all types of software applications, from office management application software and games to music and videos. Due to the enormous profit potential of selling digital products online, the industry is generally susceptible to a higher amount of fraud and fraud attempts. The risk of fraud increases as companies expand to global markets where consumer verification is limited and pirating software is a fairly common practice.

Digital River offers unique fraud avoidance services for downloadable products to help minimize the risks merchants may experience in the online purchasing environment. These services include a thorough and complex set of system-based screenings and a team of highly skilled fraud analysts who perform manual review of customer orders and report on current trends.
