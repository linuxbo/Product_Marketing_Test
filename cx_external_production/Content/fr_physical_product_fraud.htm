Fraud and Physical Products

There are two main types of fraud that are specific to physical products:

  • Shipping Fraud – Usually perpetrated by organized rings based out of Europe, Africa, Southeast Asia, and Central America. These rings present themselves as US-based individuals or as legitimate businesses in need of assistance with international drop shipping. The contact between the cyber criminal and the individual is typically made through job boards, chat rooms, or via email. Fraud rings purchase products online with stolen credit card information and use the individual’s home as the delivery address. The products are then re-shipped to the fraud ring’s location.
  • Auction Fraud – Primarily US-based and may or may not involve a fraud ring. Cyber criminals contact legitimate auction store owners and request to have their merchandise listed on their sites for a percentage of the sales profits. The criminal obtains credit card numbers from customers purchasing from the site and then purchases products from other online stores using the stolen card numbers. A cycle is established in a one-off fashion—one fraudulent transaction per credit card number. Because the cyber criminal has a continuous supply of credit card numbers, monetary losses incurred by vendors can be significant.

Fraud Reviews and Physical Inventory

When an order for a physical product is put under review for fraud, the inventory for that order is reserved until the order is released (cleared of fraud) or canceled. If the order is cleared, the order will fulfill as normal. If the order is canceled, the inventory is released and again made available on the store. Note that this does not apply to orders placed using a delayed payment method such as a check, wire transfer, money order, IBP, Konbini, Post Office, BPay, line of credit, and so on.
