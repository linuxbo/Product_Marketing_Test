Running Business Navigator Reports

Role Needed: Reporting ManagerA user role that provides access to online reports. Search this file for the "user role definitions" topic for more information.
  1. On the Reports menu, click Business Navigator. A new window opens showing the Business Navigator home page.
  2. In the list on the left, click Standard Reports.
  3. Then, click on the report grouping that contains the report you want to run. A summary of the types of reports in each group is given below:
    • Call Center Agent Reports – Call Center Agent reports show sales activity by customer service representatives. These reports are primarily used to track sales by personnel and allocate commissions on their activities.
    • Merchandising Categorization – Merchandising Categorization reports show sales, attach rates, and close ratios for bundle, cross-sell, and up-sell offers.
    • Merchandising Reports – Merchandising reports offer glimpses into various marketing activities and can help answer the question "how are people getting to my store."
    • Operational – Operational reports show you information about the current state of your demand orders (inventory levels and unfulfilled orders).
    • Product Reports – Product reports show information about sales and returns, highlighting specific product names and types of products.
    • Product Type/Product Family – Product Type/Product Family reports shows information about the custom product attributes you have created for your company and site(s).
    • Real Time Sales – Real Time Sales reports contain information about your current sales. The Real Time Customer Transaction report will show up-to-the-minute sales and the current status of demand orders. All other Real Time Sales reports show sales with a delay up to an hour.
    • Sales By Sites – The Sales by Sites report shows sales that happened on all the sites that sell your products in the channel.
    • Saved Order Reports – Saved Order Reports show information about all orders that use the "Save Order" feature that is available on some sites.
    • Subscription Reports – Subscription reports show detailed information about orders of subscription products.
    • Summary Reports – Summary reports provide a high-level view of sales activity, including both sales and traffic information.
    • Transaction Detail Reports – Transaction Detail reports provide detailed information about transactions on your site.
  4. Now, click on the name of the specific report you want to run (these will vary based on the grouping you selected above). The page refreshes to show the controls used to generate the report you have selected.
  5. Using the fields on the screen, make some selections or choices about the type of data you want in the report (such as locale, currency, or site) and the data range for the report.
  6. When you are done defining what you want the report to contain, click Run Report.
