About Copying Products

You can create one or more copies of a product, including any variations for the product. After you copy, you can modify the copied products to create new, unique products in your catalog.

Why Copy a Product?

There are many reasons why you might want to copy a product. The most common reason is to use the copied product to create a new product. This is helpful if your products are similar enough that making a copy and changing a few settings takes less time than creating each new copy individually. Once you have copied a product, you can simply return to that product and make any adjustments or changes as needed to define a new unique product.

Status of Copied Products

Copied products are placed in the Design status and must be deployed before they can be seen on your site.

What Gets Copied

When you create a copy of a product most of the attributes of the product are also be copied into the new product. If you are copying a multiple product with variations, only the base product attributes are copied into the new product (variation attributes are not copied).

Automatically Copied

The following outlines what gets copied by default when you copy an existing product:

  • Basic Product Settings (these are settings common to every product, regardless of type and fulfillment method)
    • Internal Product Name (preceded with the words "copy of" so that you can easily identify your copied products)
    • Site Product Name (preceded with the words "copy of" so that you can easily identify your copied products)
    • SKU (preceded with the words "copy of" so that you can easily identify your copied products)
    • Short Description
    • Long Description
    • Keywords
    • Thumbnail Image
    • Product Image
  • Locales (default and support) and localized product content
  • Catalog assignment (category assignment is not copied, you must assign the copied product to a category before deploying)
  • Settings for the following options and attributes:
    • Deferred Payment Override
    • Download
    • Preorder
    • Product Lifecycle (including date settings for any variations and locales)
    • Search Engine Optimization
    • Software
    • Subscription
    • Volume License Pricing

Optional to Copy

The following outlines the settings and options you can choose to copy into the new product (these appear as options when you select to copy a product):

  • Categories
  • Pricing
  • Digital rights settings (but not the actual codes used for digital rights)
  • Physical fulfillment part numbers
