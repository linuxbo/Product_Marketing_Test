Subscription Cancellations

Cancelling a subscription order is not the same as canceling the subscription itself. A subscription can be canceled at any time but an order can only be canceled under special circumstances. The following explains how a subscription can be canceled:

  • Automatic renewal of the subscription fails. Automatic renewal usually fails because payment for the renewal cannot be processed (credit card number of expiration date is wrong, or payment cannot be approved).
  • Shopper uses the self-service options to "unsubscribe immediately."
  • Customer Service uses the "unsubscribe immediately" option when viewing the subscription information for an order.
  • Integration call from a third-party or client system indicates a subscription is or should be canceled.
  • Subscription product is returned.
  • Payment for a subscription product is a "chargeback." A chargeback is when a shopper disputes they made a charge/purchase and requests to be reimbursed.

Subscription Cancellation Notifications

When a subscription is canceled, one of three emails may be sent to the shopper depending on why the subscription was canceled. The actual content and wording of these notifications can differ, based on your store has customized them.

Notification Name Reason Sent
User Subscription Cancellation - Auto Renewal Notification

Sent when the subscription could not be automatically renewed. This means there was some problem with the shopper's payment method. Usually contains information on how the shopper can manually renew.
User Subscription Cancellation - Manual Renewal Notification

Sent when the systems cancels the subscription because the shopper did not manually renew the subscription. Usually prompts the shopper to manually renew.
User Subscription Cancellation - Other Notification

Sent when the subscription is canceled prior to expiration by the shopper or Customer Service. Mainly intended to tell the shopper the subscription has been canceled per their request
