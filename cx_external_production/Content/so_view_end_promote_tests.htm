Viewing Test Results & Ending or Promoting Tests

The performance of a test can be viewed while it is live, or after is has been completed. This is critical to determine a winning test approach when making a decision about what to do with each test level.

Digital River uses a standard cumulative distribution function (CDF) to calculate the performance of each test level in the test. When viewing the details of a test, you will see a Confidence rating for each test level. Most often the level with the highest Confidence score would be promoted to your store.

Tip: If the Confidence rating for the challenger level(s) is near 0% it means that the champion (control) level is winning the test.

Promoting Test Levels

After you create a Site Optimizer Test, you can choose to promote one or more of the test levels, depending on how the level performed. When you promote a level it becomes an active offer on your site and acts like any other offer or content layout on your store. If the system detects offers that may interfere with the test you are promoting, a message will appear and a list of the conflicting offers is shown. You should retire any offers that will interfere with the test level offer you are promoting.

More about conflicting offers.

A conflicting offer is one that may interfere or affect the current test level offer you are promoting. For example, if you created a marketing offer with a discount to Product A of 50% and your test levels contain price discounts for Product A of 10% and 20%, the test levels would be superseded by the 50% discount. This condition would impact the test results, and therefore it is always advised to check conflicting offers shown to determine any possible problems that may occur with your test.

Marketing offers that are triggered by promotional URL or coupon code do not typically represent a test risk since the user must trigger the offer. However, marketing offers set to Always Triggered will be subject to best price arbitration and can therefore compete with the offers you include in your test.

When a test level is promoted, all other test level offers in the test are retired and the promoted level becomes the fully deployed offer on the site. The test will stop gathering data within Site Optimizer but will capture regular Offer ID-based tracking within the eReporting tools.

To view Site Optimizer Test results:

Role Needed: Site Optimizer1.) A feature in the Command Console that allows users to conduct site-wide A/B testing on a live site. 2.) A user role that provides access to Site Optimizer. Search this file for the "user role definitions" topic for more information.
  1. On the Marketing menu, select Site Optimizer Tests, and click Manage Site Optimizer Tests. The Site Optimizer Tests list page appears.
  2. Click on the Name of the test you want to view results for. The View Site Optimizer Test page appears.
  3. Review the test results. Based on the results of your test you may want to end the test, or if you are achieving strong results from a particular test level you can promote it to your store for all visitors to see. If you end the test, you can then retire the test to have it removed from your site. The following test information appears on the page:
    • Name – Name of the test level.
    • Offer ID – Unique ID for the marketing offer assigned to the test level.
    • Split – Percentage of shopper traffic shown each test level.
    • Control – Designates which test level is the control group for the test.
    • Unique Visits – Total number of unique visits that have been assigned to the test level.
    • Sales – Sales on a demand basis (sales that are submitted into the store). This data does not include the impact of back-orders, fraud, returns, physical shipments, etc.
    • Orders – Number of unique orders for that test level on a demand basis.
    • Close Ratio – Calculated as Carts Opened/Orders Submitted.
    • Revenue per Visitor – Calculated as Sales/Unique Visitors. The system tracks the statistical significance of the Revenue-per-Visitor measure. When a specific test level is computed to be statistically stronger than the other levels, the test level is shown in bold. This visual indication provides a way to know that the test level is winning.
    • Confidence – Indicates the level of confidence that the test level is the champion in the test. Digital River uses a standard statistical formula to determine which level is most likely the best fit for the site.
  4. To promote a test level offer, in the Levels table, select the test level you want to promote and click Promote. The Promote Site Optimizationpage appears.
    1. If there are live offers that will conflict with the offer you are promoting, a warning appears detailing the conflict. You should retire any offers that will interfere with the offer you are promoting. To retire a conflicting offer, select the offer from the list and click Retire. You can select as many offers as needed.
    2. If needed, change the Offer End Date. This is the date on which the offer will expire on your site and shoppers will no longer be able to take advantage of the offer.
    3. When you are done and ready to promote the offer, click Promote. The page refreshes and the Site Optimizer Testspage appears. The offer is now promoted and deployed to your live site. You can edit and work with this offer as you would any other marketing offer.
  5. To end the test on your site, click Terminate This Test. This will stop the test levels from appearing on your site. You can start the test again at a later time if needed.
  6. To stop the test permanently without selecting and promoting a winner, click Done. This ends all data collection and stops the test levels from being shown on the store.
