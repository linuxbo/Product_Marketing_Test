About Regulatory Fee Management

Digital River is a leading provider of global ecommerce solutions offering a comprehensive solution for regulatory fee management. Our strategic approach to fee management starts with a dedicated team who knows the laws and you – our client – ensuring that our solution meets the requirements of an ever-changing regulatory environment. Digital River’s fee management approach includes:

  • Comprehensive Fee Management Tool integrated into the Command Console to support compliance and streamline management.
  • Dedicated fee management and administration team for North America and Europe.
  • Partnership with a global consulting firm to provide legal assessments, compliance assistance, registration, and reporting services.
  • Registration in all required jurisdictions, providing capabilities to manage client obligations in all jurisdictions into which their product is shipped.

As the Reseller of Record in many markets, Digital River understands the legal obligations of these directives first-hand. We have built a strategic solution in partnership with legal experts who specialize in EC Directives and environmental law to address our obligations in each market. For clients who carry their own obligations in Europe, Digital River is prepared to offer the same streamlined solution, whatever the business model.

Fee Management Tool

Digital River’s Fee Management solution streamlines the administration and management of fees, reporting, reconciliation and remittance obligations; making compliance as easy as possible. The following is a high-level overview of the Digital River Fee Management Tool is used to manage fees:

  1. System Fee is created (and then deployed) by Digital River Fee Manager.
  2. System Fee is used (by Fee Manager, Client, or Account Manager) to create a Company Fee.
  3. Product is enabled to use fees (either individually or using the bulk tool).
  4. Company Fee is attached to product (either individually or using the bulk tool).
  5. Product is deployed and fee begins charging on the site.
  6. Fee pricing and setup is continually managed and updated as need arises.

Flexible Fee Configuration

Our Fee Management Tool comes loaded with a set of system-level fees already created and configured by our tax department. Using these fees as templates, you can create specific fees for your company in compliance with your store requirements and product line. Starting from a pre-defined system fee to create your company fees means you can be sure that the fee configuration meets the current requirements for compliance. This also means that as laws change the fee behaviors can be updated to reflect these changes.

System and Company Fees are made up of several components:

  • Custom Attributes – Defines specific qualities applied to a fee, individual product, or classification. Custom attributes can be values such as the speed of a printer or copier, or the capacity of a blank CD or DVD.
  • Classification – Defines the types of products for which a fee will be charged. Classifications can have categories and sub-categories as needed.
  • Jurisdiction – Defines the country in which the fee will be charged. Each jurisdiction can have only one country associated with it, and each country cannot be used by more than one jurisdiction.

Product and Fee Association

Once company fees are created you can enable a product to use fees and associate specific fees to those products. Fees are enabled and associated with products using the Product Editor (or the bulk tool, as explained below) in the Command Console. You choose the fee you want to associate with the product and the system does the rest. Once you deploy a product with a fee, most of the fee setup (attributes, classifications, and jurisdictions) is locked and cannot be changed.

Bulk Fee Import and Export

We built a bulk feature for fees for those clients that need to associate fees with many products at once. Using an Import and Export spreadsheet that is similar to the ones you use for other bulk catalog tasks, you can associate fees with many products as once, thus reducing the amount of time it take to get your store in fee compliance.

Reporting

Compliance schemes require a variety of reporting and fee remittance standards that vary by product, country, and fee type, and are an essential step toward total compliance. Digital River has a complete set of reports that meet both internal business management and external compliance requirements. The reports contain not only the essential data to satisfy compliance scheme reporting standards, but also an overview of fee collections and refunds to simplify the management of cash flow and accounts payable.

  • Sales and Activity Reports – Includes data required to meet the reporting standards of each recycling and copyright compliance scheme in the participating countries across Europe.
  • Accounting Reports – Facilitates the tracking and reconciliation of ensuring a more transparent accounting process.
