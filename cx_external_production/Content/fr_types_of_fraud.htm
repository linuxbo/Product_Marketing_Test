Types of Fraud

The following are the most common types of fraud used to place fraudulent orders online:

  • Skimming – Theft of magnetic-stripe credit card information compromised during a legitimate transaction. Typically an “inside job” by a dishonest employee of a legitimate merchant; can be as simple as photocopying a sales receipt.
  • Stolen Credit Card – Obtained in many ways, the simplest is copying information from retailers (online and offline). Online can involve compromising data, such as breaching a company’s credit card data warehouse. Offline involves soliciting employees with access to sensitive data in order to deliver account information in exchange for monetary compensation.
  • Phishing – Attempts to fraudulently acquire personal and financial information through fictitious communications. For example, thieves compromise passwords and credit card details by pretending to be a trustworthy person or business. The solicitation is typically a forged (imitation) email from a business or person the victim knows.
  • Pharming – Computer hackers redirect email traffic to a phony, replicated site where the user is asked for personal and financial information. Often these cases result in the download of a virus or other illegal tracking devices, including keyboard-logging programs.
  • Re-Shippers – Individuals and merchants are tricked into shipping products to countries with weak legal systems. The goods are generally paid for with stolen credit cards. Several organized fraud rings perpetrate this type of fraud, many of which are located in Eastern Europe, Asia Pacific, and Africa.
  • Fake Check Scams – Counterfeit or compromised checks are utilized to pay for goods.
  • Friendly Fraud – An acquaintance, a friend or relative of a legitimate card holder, places orders using a card or card number without the permission of the card holder.
  • Organized Fraud Rings – Highly sophisticated groups that utilize any one of the methods mentioned above to take over numerous accounts. These groups usually coordinate attacks on several merchants simultaneously and change methods and locations constantly to thwart the latest fraud prevention methods.
