Assigning Products to Catalogs and Categories

When you create a product you must assign the product to a catalog for it to appear on your store. Once a product is assigned to a catalog, you then assign it to one or more of the categories and sub-categories within that catalog.

Technically you don’t have to assign a product to a category for it to appear on your store, but we recommend you assign all products to a category because it makes it easier for shoppers to find the product in your store.

Unassigned Products

When you assign a product to a catalog, you choose which categories within that catalog you want to assign to the product. Products that are not assigned to either a catalog or category are called "unassigned" products. Products that are not assigned to a catalog differ from products not assigned to a category. The biggest difference is that products that are not assigned to a catalog cannot be purchased on your store.

  • Products not assigned to a catalog do not appear on your store and cannot be purchased by shoppers. You cannot set pricing for products that are not assigned to a catalog cannot because price lists are connected to catalogs. You must assign every product in your store to a catalog for the product to be seen or purchased on your store. To view products not assigned to a catalog you must view the Products list page and change the filter to show "All Products."
  • Products not assigned to a category within a catalog will not appear in the catalog listing pages on your store, but they can be found using the search feature. Products not assigned to a category can be purchased by shoppers on your store. To view products that are not assigned to a category you must view your list of categories and click the "Unassigned Products" link within the catalog.

Tip: We strongly recommend you assign all products to a catalog and at least one category within that catalog. You can always change which catalog and category a product is assigned to by editing the product or viewing your list of categories.
