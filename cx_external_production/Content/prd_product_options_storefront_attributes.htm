Storefront Settings Attributes

The following table outlines all the attributes you can set in the Storefront Settings area:

Attribute

Definition

Internal Product Name

Name of the product that is used internally in the Command Console. This name does not appear on your store or to shoppers.

Site Product Name

Name of the product as you want it to appear on your store and seen by shoppers. This name is part of what is searched when shoppers search your store.

SKU

The SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) is a code (letters, numbers, symbols, or combination) that uniquely identifies a specific product. Unlike the Product ID, which is generated by the system, your company creates this ID.

External Reference Number

The code (letters, numbers, symbols, or combination) that uniquely identifies a product to a third party. Unlike the Product ID (which is generated) or the SKU (which may not be unique) the third party decides what identifies this product.

Manufacturer Name

Name of the company that manufacturers the product.

Manufacturer Part Number

Number the manufacturer uses to identify the product.

Tax Group

 Grouping used to determine the specific tax types you can select for the product.
Tax Type Specific tax category for the product used to calculate tax.

Return Type

Indicates whether the shopper must physically send the product back or if they must comply with an ELOD and delete the software they purchased.

Physical Product – Indicates that the product must be physically returned to the fulfiller or store.

Electronic Letter of Destruction (ELOD) – Indicates that an ELOD must be completed for the product to be returned.

Options

Is Viewable – Indicates if the product can be seen on your store when a shopper browses a category or performs a search.

Is Orderable – Indicates if the product can be ordered on your store.

Short Description

Used to describe the features, benefits, and so on for the product.

The Short Description appears on your store when the product is seen in the Category Listing and Search Results pages.

The Short Description also appears when a shopper views the Product Details overlay within the Shopping Cart, or when the shopper sees a list of "Recently Viewed Products" on your store (not all stores are set to show recently viewed products).

You can use HTML in the Short Description field to customize the appearance of this text when it is seen on your store.

Long Description

Used to describe the features, benefits, and so on for the product.

By default, the Long Description appears on the Product Detail page when shoppers view a product.

You can use HTML in the Long Description field to customize the appearance of this text when it is seen on your store.

Keywords

Words that can be used to describe the product. When shoppers search your store, the result they get are based on the keywords you have entered for the product, in addition to any words in the Short and Long Descriptions.

Thumbnail Image

Image that appears for the product when the product is seen on a listing page, such as when searches are performed or the contents of a category are viewed.

Product Image

Image that appears for the product when the shopper views the Product Detail page on your store.

Email Settings

Used to enter additional text that you want to appear in the "Order Confirmation" email that is sent to shoppers when they submit an order.

Text-Based Email – Raw text version of the additional text or information you want to appear in the "Order Confirmation" email that is sent to a shopper see when they submit an order.

HTML-Based Email – HTML formatted version of the additional text or information you want to appear in the "Order Confirmation" email that is sent to a shopper see when they submit an order.

Thank You Page

Thank You Message – Additional text or information you want to appear on the "Thank You" page that shoppers see when they submit an order.

Minimum/Maximum

Used to set minimum and maximum purchase quantity limits for the product. Minimum and maximum settings are enforced per order and shoppers may place multiple orders to get around purchase limits.

Minimum Quantity – Indicates if a shopper must order a minimum number of the product in each order. To have no restrictions you would simply leave the field blank or enter 0 (zero).

Maximum Quantity – Indicates if a shopper can order only a certain number of the product in each order. To have no restrictions you would simply leave the field blank or enter 0 (zero).
