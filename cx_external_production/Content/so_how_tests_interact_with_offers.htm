How Site Optimizer Interacts with Other Offers

Many Site Optimizer tests are created to evaluate two or more marketing offers. Unfortunately, your store cannot differentiate between offers created for a Site Optimizer test and regular offers you have created for your shoppers. This means that the results of a Site Optimizer test can be impacted by offers that are outside the test cells.

For example, let’s say your store has an "Always Triggered" offer that gives a 20% discount on Product A. You create a Site Optimizer test that contains two test levels to offer Product A at a 10% discount and a 15% discount. When a shopper attempts to purchase Product A, they would receive the 20% discount because shoppers always get the best discount when a store has conflicting offers (see How Conflicting Offers are Treated). As a result, your test can’t be accurately evaluated because other offers are affecting the outcome.

When you create a Site Optimizer test, the system will notify you if there are existing offers that may affect the outcome of the test. We recommend that you pay close attention to these notices when you complete the final step of the creation process. You may decide to retire the conflicting offers or delay the test until the other offers are retired.

Tip: Marketing offers that use the "Coupon Code" or "Promotional URL" triggers do not usually pose a risk to the test because a shopper can only trigger these offers by entering a coupon code or clicking a link. However, offers that are set to "Always Triggered" are subject to offer arbitration and can therefore compete with the offers you include in your test. See How Conflicting Offers are Treated for more about offer arbitration.
