Credit Card Association Fraud Prevention Tools

Some of the tools Digital River uses to help identify and stop fraud are actually tools that the credit card companies have developed and maintain. The credit card tools provide some relief for online stores but they also increase the chances of a valid order from a legitimate customer being misidentified as fraud. This is why Digital River employs other tools and technologies to detect and fix fraud.

The following outlines the credit card tools Digital River uses to detect and stop fraud:

  • Address Verification System (AVS) – Validates address information from the online transaction with the credit card issuing-bank’s records. The AVS is susceptible to inconsistencies in data entry between the ecommerce forms and the information on file with the card network. When used alone, AVS may cause legitimate orders to be rejected (also referred to as false positives or customer insults).
  • Card Verification Codes (CVC) – A credit/debit card security feature. A unique identifier in the form of a numeric code assigned to each newly-issued card. CVCs are intended to provide validation that the card is physically present during the transaction and can help determine if the card is legitimate or counterfeit. CVCs also are referred to as:
    • CVV2 (Visa)
    • CVC2 (MasterCard)
    • CID (American Express, Discover)
    • CAV (JCB)
  • Verified by Visa (VbV)/MasterCard Secure Code (MCSC) – Also known as 3D-Secure, this tool authenticates the cardholder during checkout. Cardholders are required to provide a unique password validated by banks through real-time online verification. Authenticated transactions are subsequently protected from a merchant’s financial liability by the credit card associations.

Despite credit card associations’ efforts to contain online fraud, these prevention tools are less than perfect and fraud continues to increase at unprecedented rates.
