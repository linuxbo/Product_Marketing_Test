Viewing Site Trends (Legacy)

The legacy Site Trends pod shows information about traffic and orders for the currently selected site. If your company has more than one site, you will see a comparison of the currently selected site (called the primary site) with another site owned by your company.

To view Site Trends (legacy):

Role Needed: Reporting ManagerA user role that provides access to online reports. Search this file for the "user role definitions" topic for more information. or Site PulseA user role that provides access to Site Trends. Search this file for the "user role definitions" topic for more information.
  • There are two views for Site Trends: a summary view that appears on the Home page and a detailed view that appears when you click the View More Detail Link.

Summary View

On the Home page, you will see a summary view of the Site Trends. See example.

  • Site Trends – The summary view (on the Home page) shows different metrics about orders and performance of your store for the last seven days. Red and green arrows indicate the increase or decrease for the metric from the current seven day period to the previous seven day period.
  • Sales – The summary view (on the Home page) shows a line chart of Gross Sales and Demand Sales for the last sixty days, overlaid together to provide more insight about sales performance on your store. To change the time period you are viewing, click on a circle in the line chart to drill in and view data for that to that date and change what is shown on the chart.

Detailed View

Click the View More Detail link in the summary view to get a more detailed view (you can also click Site Pulse on the Reports menu to see the detailed views). See the information further in this topic about interacting with the detailed view. See examples.

 

  • Site Trends – The detailed view shows how the data for the currently selected (primary) site compares to itself within time periods.
  • Sales – The detailed view shows a side-by-side comparison of Gross Sales and Demand Orders, a “Period Comparison” for orders, and graphs showing performance of the “Top 5 Products” and “Top 5 Programs" on the site. You can change what you see in the detailed view by changing which charts appear on the page and which time frame is used to show data.

Understanding the Data

Site Trends shows various metrics for sales and traffic on your site(s) in the currency set as the default for the company (not the site). The following outlines the metrics you can see when you view either Site Trends or Sales.

Metric Definition
Average Order Value (AOV) Average value for all orders reported by demand sales.
Baskets Number of baskets (shopping carts) opened. A cart is opened when a shopper adds at least one product to their cart.
Close Ratio Number of orders placed on the site for demand sales. This is calculated by dividing the number of shopping carts (baskets) that were opened (a basket is opened when at least one product is added to the shopping cart) by the number of orders placed.
Demand Sales Number of orders placed that cleared fraud.
Gross Sales Total sales for the indicated time period. Gross sales are defined as total sales, not including returns or refunds. This can also be thought of as recognized revenue for the period (fulfilled sales).
Orders Total value of orders placed.
Unique Visits Number of unique visitors.

The Detailed View

The detailed Site Trends data view appears when you click View More Detail on the Home page, or when you click Site Pulse on the Reports menu. On the detailed view page you will also see the top give products and top five programs for your site.

The following are some helpful tips for interacting and working with the detailed views.

  • In the Sales Data area, click Settings to change what metrics (Gross Sales, Demand Sales, and so on) are shown in the graphs or to change the time period being show.

  • In the Sales Data view, you can also click and drag on the anchors that appear in the area below the charts to change the focus.


  • Use the links in the upper-right corner of either the Sales Data or Site Trends areas to choose a pre-defined time period to view.

  • If your company has multiple sites, the Site ID list will appear so you can select which site you want to view.
  • Hover the cursor on an area of the Sales Data graph to show the value for that time period for the metric shows. You can tell which time period you are viewing because of the circle becomes colored in on the chart.

  • The Top 5 Product and Top 5 Programs snapshots show a graphical snapshot of the top five selling products and top 5 redeemed offers on your site. Includes demand sales and shipped sales for both offers and products.
