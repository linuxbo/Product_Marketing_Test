About Product Attribute Families

Product Attribute Families are how a product is defined for your store and in some way how the product data is structured and saved in our database.

Note: The information in this section is provided for educational reference only. You do not need to have a thorough understanding of Product Attribute Families to create and manage your products in the Command Console.

A Product Attribute Family is just that: a family of related attributes that are used to describe a product. For example, families are often to used to define characteristics such as size, color, version, platform, and so on. When you create a product, you choose which settings you want the product to assume and many of those settings are actually Product Attribute Families.

You can think of Attribute Families as any way you can categorize, label, organize, or define your products. Most of the settings and options you set up for a product are actually Product Attribute Families and there are two kinds used to define products on Digital River stores: Custom Attribute Families and Common Attribute Families.

Custom Attribute Families

A Custom Attribute Family is a family that has been created specifically for your store and for you to use with your products. Custom Attribute Families are created by Digital River, usually when you store is initially created. To learn more about any Custom Attribute Families created for your store (or to learn if you can get Custom Attribute Families created), contact your Store Operations team.

Common Attribute Families

A Common Attribute Family is a family that is available to all stores hosted by Digital River. Common Attribute Families are the settings and attributes most commonly used by our clients to define products. There are several Common Attribute Families used to define products for your store. For more information about these families, see Product Options.
